🎯 Week 2 Target Map — One Good Strike Ends It
👃
NOSE
Palm heel strike or tight jab · 1 or 2
Instant involuntary response — eyes water, vision blurs, pain reflex makes them flinch back. Doesn't need much force. Most fights end here.
Primary
🫁
SOLAR PLEXUS
Sharp cross or 1-2 · aim just below the chest, above belly button
Knocks the wind out — diaphragm spasms, they can't breathe for several seconds. Can't fight when you can't breathe. Especially effective on kids with less muscle.
Primary
🫦
CHIN / JAW
Cross (2) or hook (3) · clean contact
Head snaps back, blood rushes from brain, immediate disorientation and dizziness. Classic fight-stopper. Requires clean contact — the 1-2-3 combo sets this up.
Secondary
🦵
SHIN
Teep / push kick · or hard stamp to lower shin
Sharp shin pain creates instant distance. Not a fight-ender alone — but combined with the combo it backs them up hard and creates the space to step back into ready position.
Support
❌ Never: Temple · Throat · Back of head · Someone on the ground
✅ One strike. Right target. Step back. Ready position. Done.

Uncle Adam's Doctrine for Lucas Reinhard · Holiday 2026
📐 Program Design
  • 4 sessions/day — Learn (S1) · Drill (S2) · Bag (S3) · Game (S4)
  • 15–20 min each — a 6-year-old has 20 minutes of peak focus. Use it all, waste none.
  • 2h gaps minimum — recovery IS training. The muscles rebuild between sessions.
  • ONE focus per session — deep over wide. Mastery, not exposure.
  • Fitness Beast Days: Day 5 & Day 12 — no bag, pure conditioning. Animal circuits, sprints, star jumps, planks. Build the engine.
🥊 Technique Doctrine (Adam's Method)
  • HIGH JABS — arm fully extended ABOVE the head. Left × 30, right × 30. Non-negotiable daily drill.
  • Legs First — teep is the primary weapon. One push kick = 2 metres of distance. Punches are secondary.
  • The Warrior Spin — push from behind → STOMP → spin → hands up → STOP → teep. 5 steps. Automatic.
  • Combos — 1=jab, 2=cross, 3=hook. 1-2 · 1-1-2 · 1-2-3. Build in that order.
  • Footwork — shuffle, pivot, L-step. Distance = safety. Never cross the feet.

"You have pushed me every single day since Kindergarten for NO reason. STOP."

From the moment you see the threat — until you are walking away safe. Every moment in between: ready.

✅ If they go down — YOU STOP HITTING.
Step back. Hands up. Stance. Eyes locked. Stay READY — they may get back up.
A warrior does not strike someone on the ground. Ever. But a warrior stays ready until the threat is gone.

✅ You might get hit. It's OK. Keep going.
✅ There may be blood. It's OK. It means it worked.
✅ Your hand may hurt. It's OK. Wrist locked. Punch through.

The first fight embarrasses him. His dad will send him back. This is not failure — this is expected.

"I told you last time. Don't fight me. I WILL fight back."

Consistent. Controlled. No anger. No chasing.
