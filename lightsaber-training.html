FOOTWORK = EVERYTHING
Muhammad Ali said: "Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee."
The greatest fighters in history all had one thing in common — incredible feet.
A Jedi never stands still.
Step 1 — The Warrior Stance
⚡ THE PERFECT STANCE
Learn this first. Everything else builds on it.
🧍
SIDE VIEW
FEET
Shoulder-width apart
Use the "High & Heavy" test — rock side to side until perfect
LEAD
Left foot forward (if right-handed)
45° angle — pointing toward your opponent
BACK
Right foot slightly turned out
45° angle — your power foot, drives all attacks
KNEES
ALWAYS slightly bent
Power comes from legs — never lock your knees
HIPS
Over the arch of your foot
Not too forward, not too back — centred over arch
CHEST
Slightly forward of hips
Never lean back — forward = power and balance
WEIGHT
Balls of your feet — NEVER flat
Like a spring, ready to explode in any direction
EYES
Chin DOWN, eyes UP
Always watching your opponent — never look at your feet
🎯 THE HIGH & HEAVY TEST
1. Stand straight, feet shoulder-width
2. Rock slowly side to side
3. Widen feet until you can barely rock — that's HEAVY
4. Bring feet back in until you feel TALL again but still HEAVY
5. That's your perfect stance width!
Step 2 — The 5 Fundamental Moves
➡️ ADVANCE (Move Forward)
For attacking
Lead foot moves FIRST → back foot follows the same distance.

Never bring your feet together — always stay shoulder-width.
Small steps. Stay low. Stay bouncy.
🎯 DRILL: Take 3 advance steps → stop → reset. Feet never touching or crossing. × 20
⬅️ RETREAT (Move Back)
For defending — never turn your back!
Back foot moves FIRST → lead foot follows the same distance.

Keep facing your opponent the whole time.
Retreating = staying in control, NOT running away.
🎯 DRILL: 3 retreats → 3 advances → back to start. Never crossing feet. × 15
↔️ LATERAL STEP (Move Sideways)
The most powerful move — hard to attack someone moving sideways
Move the foot closest to the direction first.
Going left → left foot moves first.
Going right → right foot moves first.

Stay parallel. Never cross your feet. Stay in stance the whole time.
🎯 DRILL: Step left × 3 → step right × 3 → repeat. Stay low. × 20
🔄 THE PIVOT (Spin & Face)
Change direction in a flash — confuse your opponent
Pivot on your lead foot. Back foot swings around.

Used for: The Warrior Spin, escaping an attack, repositioning.
Stay on the BALL of your lead foot — never the heel.
Knees bent the whole time.
🎯 DRILL: Pivot left 90° → return → pivot right 90° → return. × 20. The Warrior Spin uses this!
⚡ THE LUNGE (Attack!)
Explosive advance for a power strike
Drive off the back foot → lead foot shoots forward → strike as you land.

Like a spring releasing. All power comes from the back leg.
Recover immediately — don't leave yourself open after the lunge.
🎯 DRILL: Lunge forward + strike → immediately retreat 2 steps → reset. × 15
Step 3 — The Golden Rules
🏆 NEVER BREAK THESE RULES
❌
NEVER cross your feet.
You'll lose balance instantly. One push and you fall.
❌
NEVER stand flat-footed.
Heels down = slow, heavy, easy to push over.
❌
NEVER stand still.
A still target is an easy target. Always bouncing.
❌
NEVER lock your knees.
Straight knees = no power, no speed, no bounce.
❌
NEVER look at your feet.
Eyes always on the opponent. Feel the ground with your feet.
✅
ALWAYS know where your feet are.
Great fighters feel the floor without looking at it.
✅
ALWAYS move the right foot first.
Going forward → lead foot first. Going back → back foot first.
✅
ALWAYS stay bouncy.
Ali called it floating. Light feet = fast feet.
Step 4 — Footwork Drills
🔵 Drill — The Box Drill
The most important footwork drill in any martial art
Imagine a box on the floor (4 corners).

Advance to front-left corner → lateral step to front-right → retreat to back-right → lateral step back to start.

Stay in perfect stance the whole time. Never crossing. Always bouncing.
🟠 Drill — Advance & Retreat × 20
Build the muscle memory that lasts a lifetime
Start in perfect stance.
3 advances → 3 retreats → back to start.

Count out loud. Lead foot ALWAYS first on advance. Back foot ALWAYS first on retreat.
🟢 Drill — The Ali Shuffle
Muhammad Ali's secret weapon — fast feet confuse everyone
Bounce lightly on balls of feet.
Quickly switch lead foot → right foot forward → left foot forward → right foot forward.

Like hopping but staying in the same spot. Fast and light.
This is what Uncle teaches — fast feet = fast brain!
🟣 Drill — Pivot Warrior Spin
Combine footwork with the Warrior Spin — non-negotiable
Start in stance.
Someone approaches from behind — STOMP → pivot on lead foot → back foot swings around → face them → HANDS UP → say "STOP!"

The footwork IS the Warrior Spin. Practice it until it's automatic.
🚨 DAILY MISSION — Ankle & Balance
⚡ UNCLE'S ORDER — DO THIS EVERY SINGLE DAY
Lucas's ankle needs to get strong. Right now it wobbles on one leg — that means every stance, every pivot, every Warrior Spin is built on a weak foundation.

We fix this first. Everything else comes after.
🦶 Level 1 — Single Leg Stand
The starting point. Do this until it feels easy.
Stand on ONE foot. Other foot slightly lifted.
Hold as long as possible without wobbling.

Week 1 target: 10 seconds
Week 2 target: 20 seconds
Week 3 target: 30 seconds
Week 4 target: 30 seconds — EYES CLOSED

Do both feet. Left AND right. 3 times each foot per day.
🏋️ Level 2 — Single Leg Calf Raises
Builds ankle strength directly. This is the exercise.
Stand on ONE foot near a wall (can touch for safety at first).
Rise up onto your tiptoe — hold 1 second — lower slowly.

Start: 5 raises each foot
Week 2: 10 raises each foot
Week 3: 15 raises each foot, NO wall
Week 4: 20 raises each foot, NO wall, EYES CLOSED

Go SLOW on the way down — that's where the strength comes from.
🔵 Level 3 — Ankle Alphabet
Builds ankle mobility in all directions
Sit on a chair. Lift one foot off the ground.
Write the alphabet A → Z in the air using ONLY your ankle.
Make each letter as BIG as possible.

This makes the ankle strong in every direction — not just up-down.
Do both feet. Takes about 2 minutes total.
⚡ Level 4 — One-Leg Hops
When Level 1 is easy — this is the next challenge
Hop on ONE foot — same spot, small hops.
Land softly each time — absorb the landing through your ankle and knee.

Start: 5 hops each foot
Week 3: 10 hops each foot, forward-back
Week 4: 10 hops each foot, side-to-side

SOFT LANDING IS THE KEY — thud = wrong. Quiet = right.
🎯 Level 5 — Pillow Balance Challenge
Uncle's hardest challenge — only for when Level 1 is solid
Put a cushion or folded towel on the floor.
Stand on it with ONE foot.
The unstable surface forces the ankle to work harder.

Target: Hold 30 seconds each foot without falling

This is what professional athletes use. It's called "proprioception training" — teaching the ankle to automatically balance.

💜 The Dancer Secret
The best fighters trained like dancers
Muhammad Ali danced. Sugar Ray Robinson danced. Lomachenko danced.

Dancers have the best footwork of any athlete on earth. They train balance from age 4. Ballet dancers can spin on one foot, leap 3 metres, and land perfectly.

That's the secret: train your feet like a dancer, fight like a warrior.

How to train like a dancer:
• Slow motion footwork drills — control first, speed second
• Single-leg balance holds — 30 seconds each leg
• Skipping — best footwork trainer ever invented
• Bare feet on grass — build feel and awareness
